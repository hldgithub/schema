Appendix D

YOUNG COMPENSATION INVENTORY


Instructions:

Listed below are statements that a person might use to describe himself or herself. Please read each statement and decide how well it describes you.

If you desire, reword the statement so that the statement would be even more true of you. Then choose the highest rating from 1 to 6 that describes you (including your revisions), and write the number in the space before the statement.


Rating Scale:

1 = Completely untrue of me

2 = Mostly untrue of me

3 = Slightly more true than untrue

4 = Moderately true of me

5 = Mostly true of me

6 = Describes me perfectly

SCORING

To score this inventory, we simply circle all items with a high rating of "5" or "6". These represent the ways the patient overcompensates for emotions connected with his or her schemas. We discuss each of these high-rated items with the patient, asking for examples of when the patient can remember using this compensatory strategy. We then try to help the patient identify which schemas the patient is compensating for through these coping strategies.

The same form of overcompensation could be used to cope with many different schemas. Thus, there is not a one-to-one correspondence between each item on the YCI and any schema. However, the table below lists some of the most common schemas associated with each of the items on the YCI, based on our clinical observation.
